### Mac Addresses

Media Access Controll Addresss (MAC)

- MAC Addresses operate at Layer 2 (Data Link Layer)
- A MAC Address is 48 bits long
- The first 24 bits are the OUI (Organizationally Unique Identifier)
- The last 24 bits are the Station Address identifying the device
- MAC Addresses are written as six groups of two hexadecimal digits separated by colons, hyphens, or no separator
- MAC Addresses are assigned by the **NIC manufacturer** not the ISP
- The address is typically stored (burned) into the network interface hardware (NIC)
